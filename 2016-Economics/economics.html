Economic Concepts

budget

an estimate, often itemised, of expected income and expenses for a given period in the future

example: now I could, should, and would buy the fanta, but instead I'll buy the 89 cent 1.25 litre budget stuff.

inflation

a persistent, substantial rise in the general level of prices related to an increase in the volume of money and resulting in the loss of value of currency

example: the government flooding a country with money to pay off its war debts.

savings

Income not spent

interest

a share, right, or title in the ownership of property, in a commercial or financial undertaking

example:putting money into a bank, and then letting that money sit there, which gains you money from the bank... well, not the bank, the idiots who think they can take a loan without going bankrupt.

money

any circulating medium of exchange, including coins, paper money, and demand deposits.

example: Coins

banks

a place to store money in.

example: your local westpack, or kiwibank, or asb, or anz, or rabobank, or tsb... ok theres a lot of them and theyre all stupidly rich.

investing

to put (money) to use, by purchase or expenditure, in something offering potential profitable returns, as interest, income, or appreciation in value

example: investing into the wheel because you think its a cool idea.

opportunity cost

spending money on something at the cost of not being able to buy something else.

example: so I could buy wayyyy too many healing potions, OR I could buy a peice or armor for +2 defense...

scarcity

Limited means, Unlimited Wants

exchange

trading someone an object (money or otherwise) for something they also want to trade.

example: a glock candy apple for a nova candy apple

consumers

people who buy stuff.

example: you and me

supply

the amount of something (whether it be 50 caliber rounds or doughnuts)

example:500 loaves of bread

demand

Demand is an economic principle that describes a consumer's desire and willingness to pay a price for a specific good or service. Holding all other factors constant, the price of a good or service increases as its demand increases and vice versa.
